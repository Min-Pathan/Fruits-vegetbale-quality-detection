Machine learning algorithms can be trained to detect and localize fruits and vegetables within images. This involves training a machine learning model, such as a convolutional neural network (CNN), on a large dataset of annotated images. This system can be trained to assess the quality of fruits and vegetables based on visual characteristics such as size, color, shape, and texture. By analyzing these features, the models can classify the produce into different quality grades or identify defects and blemishes. This information is valuable for farmers, suppliers, and food processors to ensure that only high-quality products reach the market.
